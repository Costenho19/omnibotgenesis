1. Purpose

The parties wish to explore a technical collaboration involving a structured architectural crosswalk between CAI-EXPERT-LAB and the OMNIX Agent Trust Fabric (ATF), including comparative analysis of governance invariants, runtime admissibility models, semantic drift protocols, and cross-framework interoperability. In connection with this collaboration, each party may disclose confidential information to the other party.



2. Definition of Confidential Information

Confidential Information means any technical, architectural, commercial, or operational information disclosed by one party to the other in connection with the collaboration, including but not limited to:

- Protocol specifications, field definitions, invariant sets, and trace formats
- API architecture, endpoint specifications, and governance logic
- Authority chain design, cryptographic methods, and receipt structures
- Runtime governance models, continuity mechanisms, and admissibility logic
- Business strategy, commercial terms, and partner relationships
- Any information marked as confidential or that a reasonable party would understand to be confidential given the context of disclosure

For the avoidance of doubt: the OMNIX ATF invariants (ATF-INV-001 through ATF-INV-006), the Delegation Receipt (DR), Temporal Admissibility Record (TAR), and Runtime Continuity Record (RCR) structures, and all associated governance logic are the intellectual property of OMNIX QUANTUM LTD and constitute Confidential Information of Party B.



3. Obligations

Each party agrees to:

- Hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of the architectural crosswalk collaboration described in Section 1
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
- Limit access to Confidential Information to personnel who have a need to know for the collaboration
- Apply at minimum the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, and no less than reasonable care
- Not incorporate, adapt, or derive work products from the other party's Confidential Information outside the scope of this collaboration without explicit written authorisation



4. Exclusions

Obligations under this Agreement do not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement (including information published under DOI by either party prior to this Agreement)
- Was rightfully known to the receiving party prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed by the receiving party without use of Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided the disclosing party is given reasonable prior notice



5. Intellectual Property

Nothing in this Agreement transfers any intellectual property rights between the parties. Each party retains full ownership of its existing intellectual property. Disclosure of Confidential Information does not grant any licence, right, or interest in the disclosing party's intellectual property.

Any joint output produced under this collaboration (including crosswalk documents, comparative analyses, or joint publications) requires prior written approval from both parties before publication or external distribution.



6. Term and Termination

This Agreement is effective from the date above and continues for two (2) years. Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days written notice. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for a period of three (3) years.



7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with applicable law. Both parties agree to resolve disputes through good faith negotiation before pursuing any formal legal process.



8. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements relating to confidentiality between the parties.
