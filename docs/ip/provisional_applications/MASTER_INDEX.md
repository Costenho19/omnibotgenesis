## STEP-BY-STEP FILING GUIDE

### Step 1: Create USPTO Account
1. Go to https://patentcenter.uspto.gov
2. Click "Create Account"
3. Complete registration as an individual inventor (Pro Se)
4. Verify email address

### Step 2: Verify Micro Entity Status
Before filing, confirm you meet ALL of the following criteria:
- [ ] You qualify as a small entity (gross income does not exceed 3× the US median household income, approximately $213,000 as of 2024)
- [ ] You have NOT been named as an inventor on more than 4 previously filed US patent applications (excluding those assigned to an employer)
- [ ] You have NOT assigned, licensed, or otherwise obligated yourself to assign or license any rights in the invention to an entity that does not qualify as a micro entity

If you are uncertain about your micro entity status, consult a registered patent attorney before selecting this status, as false certification can invalidate your patent.

### Step 3: Prepare Documents for Each Family
For EACH of the three families (file separately):
1. **Convert the Specification to PDF** — USPTO accepts PDF format
2. **Convert the Cover Sheet to PDF**
3. **Add today's date** to all [DATE OF FILING] placeholders
4. **Add your address and email** to all placeholder fields
5. **Sign the Cover Sheet declaration** (digital signature accepted in USPTO Patent Center)

### Step 4: File Each Application in USPTO Patent Center
1. Log in to https://patentcenter.uspto.gov
2. Click "File a new application"
3. Select: Provisional Application
4. Upload the Cover Sheet PDF
5. Upload the Specification PDF (including drawings)
6. Select entity status: Micro Entity
7. Pay filing fee: $320.00
8. Submit and download the filing receipt

**File each of the three families as a SEPARATE application. Do not combine them.**

### Step 5: Record Filing Receipts
After each filing, USPTO will provide:
- Application number (format: 63/XXX,XXX)
- Filing date (official priority date)
- Filing receipt PDF

Record all three application numbers and filing dates here:

## WHAT HAPPENS AFTER FILING

1. **Immediately:** You have "Patent Pending" status. You may publicly disclose the invention.
2. **Within 12 months:** File nonprovisional (utility) application claiming priority to this provisional.
3. **12 months after nonprovisional filing:** USPTO begins examination.
4. **2–4 years after nonprovisional filing (typical):** USPTO issues office actions; prosecution begins.
5. **3–5 years after nonprovisional filing (typical):** Patent granted (if prosecution successful).

## IMPORTANT LEGAL NOTICES

1. **This package does not constitute legal advice.** The inventor is strongly advised to consult with a registered patent attorney or patent agent (USPTO registered) before or after filing.

2. **Micro entity certification:** Filing with false micro entity status is a serious matter that can affect patent validity. Verify eligibility carefully.

3. **12-month deadline is absolute:** Unlike some legal deadlines, the 12-month deadline for filing a nonprovisional application claiming benefit of a provisional cannot be extended.

4. **Public disclosure:** Filing the provisional establishes the priority date and allows public disclosure. However, public disclosure before filing permanently bars patenting in most countries other than the US. Since this filing is being made now, future international filings should be made within 12 months of this provisional's filing date.
